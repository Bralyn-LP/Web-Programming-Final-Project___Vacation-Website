Central Europe Package

Featuring: Germany, Netherlands, Switzerland

Germany

Embark on a journey through the picturesque landscapes and cultural treasures of Germany.

Netherlands

Uncover the enchanting wonders of the Netherlands, where tulip fields and historic windmills blend seamlessly with vibrant cities and rich heritage.

Switzerland

Experience the timeless allure of Switzerland, where majestic Alps and pristine lakes create a perfect harmony of nature and tradition.


Black Forest

Take a hike through the forested mountains of the Black Forest; beautiful treelines, and quaint villages abound.

With both short trails, and long trails up to 130km these woods contain trails for all interested in a journey.


Brandenburg Gate

Come see the Brandenburg Gate an 18th century monument of Berlin constructed on the site of a former city gate.

The Brandenburg Gate was the site of several historic events, and with the division created by the Berlin Wall was within a restricted area made inaccessible to both those of the east and west. Upon its reopening the Brandenburg Gate became a symbol of unity.


Neuschwanstein Castle

Visit the Neuschwanstein Castle; a 19th century castle resting on a rugged hill in the Alps.

Restoration work began in 2017, and due to the nature of the restoration only guided tours are currently avaible. The estimated completion for restorations is spring 2024.


Van Gohg Museum

With more than 200 painting, 500 drawings, and 700 letters in its collection the Van Gohg Museum as its name implies is home to the largest collection of Van Gohg's works in the world.

Multiple-day tickets are avaible and encouraged, as for many the size of this collection is not feasible to take in within a single visit.


The windmills of Kinderdjik

Observe one of the largest collections of 18th century windmills on this tour of Kinderdjik, a small Dutch village located at the meeting point of two rivers.

Be guided along the paths of the 19 remaining Windmills of Kinderdjik and learn about the extensive canal system of Kinderdjik.


Amsterdam

Visit Amsterdam, the capital of the Netherlands and admire its canal system while spending time at the local stores.


The Matterhorn

Come and trek the Matterhorn the massive mountain of the Alps straddling the border of Italy and Switzerland.

The full hike along the mountains is a 5 day journey of 4-8 hour intervals! For the sake of time shorter treks are offered/recommended and will be required to work alongside this travel plan.


Jungfraujoch

Famous for being the highest accessible railway in Europe Jungfraujoch requires a 35 minute journy on the Jungfraujoch Railway ascending 1400 metres in just 35 minutes!.

There are many other sub-attractions at Jungfraujoch including an observation deck, Ice Palace, The Snow Park, and the Lindt Swiss Chocolate Heaven.

Chapel Bridge

The Chapel Bridge or the Kapellbrücke is a woodenfoot bridge spanning the river Reuss. Come sightsee at this historic Swiss landmark and cross the river into town.

Dating back to the 17th century the Chapel Bridge itself is a piece of history, and within it lies more history to behold as it bares paintings from the 17th century depicting the history of the bridge.
